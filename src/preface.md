# Preface

**Shoonya** is a research-driven, highly optimized transformer-based language model implementing cutting-edge techniques from 2024-2025 research. It achieves 100-500x effective compute efficiency through advanced optimizations including FlashAttention-3, Mixture of Experts, Mamba/SSM hybrid blocks, KV cache quantization, and speculative decoding.

This book documents the architecture, design decisions, and research behind Shoonya — from the foundational concepts of transformers and attention, through the model architecture and training pipeline, to production inference serving and the frontier of graph-aware embeddings.

## Who is this book for?

*   **ML Engineers** who want to understand the internals of a modern LLM implementation
*   **Researchers** exploring the intersection of language models, graph databases, and embeddings
*   **Practitioners** building production inference pipelines with PyTorch, vLLM, and quantization
*   **The curious** who want to know what transformers actually are and why they work

## About the Project

Shoonya is part of the VaidhyaMegha ecosystem, alongside the Samyama Graph Database. Together, they aim to bridge the gap between unstructured language understanding and structured knowledge representation — a gap that current AI systems have yet to fully close.

The name "Shoonya" (शून्य) means "zero" or "void" in Sanskrit — representing both the starting point from which all computation emerges, and the aspiration toward models that achieve maximum capability from minimal compute.
